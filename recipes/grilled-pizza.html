Grilled Pizza

Description

This on-the-grill pizza recipe includes home-made dough and room to add all of your favorite pizza toppings!

Estimated time to make: 3 hours

Ingredients

    Dough:

  • 1 cup of warm water
  • .25 ounce of active dry yeast
  • 1 pinch of white sugar
  • 3.5 cups of all-purpose flower
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons of kosher salt
  • 2 cloves of minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon of chopped fresh basil

    • Garlic Oil:

  • .5 cup of olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon of minced garlic

    • Toppings:

  • .25 cup of tomato sauce, divided
  • 1 cup of chopped tomatoes, divided
  • 2 cups of shredded mozzarella cheese, divided
  • 4 tablespoons of chopped fresh basil, divided
  • Any other toppings of your choice!

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.

    2. Making Dough:

  2. Pour warm water into a large bowl; dissolve yeast and sugar in warm water. Let stand until yeast softens and begins to form a creamy foam, about 5 to 10 minutes.
  3. Mix in flour, 1 tablespoon olive oil, and salt until dough pulls away from the sides of the bowl.
  4. Turn onto a lightly floured surface. Knead until smooth, about 8 minutes.
  5. Place dough in a well-oiled bowl and cover with a damp cloth.
  6. Set aside to rise until doubled, about 1 hour. Punch down; knead in garlic and basil. Set aside to rise for 1 more hour, or until doubled again.

    7. Meanwhile, Making Garlic Oil:

  7. Combine 1/2 cup olive oil with minced garlic in a microwave-safe cup or bowl. Heat for 30 seconds in the microwave.
  8. Preheat an outdoor grill for high heat; brush the grate with garlic oil.

    9. Making Pizza:

  9. Punch down dough and divide in half. Form each half into an oblong shape 3/8 to 1/2 inch thick.
  10. Carefully place one piece of dough on the hot grill. Dough will begin to puff almost immediately. When the bottom crust has lightly browned, turn dough over using two spatulas.
  11. Working quickly, brush garlic oil over crust.
  12. Top with 1/2 of each of the following: tomato sauce, chopped tomatoes, cheese, basil, and any other topping(s) of your preference.
  13. Close the lid and cook until cheese melts. Remove from grill and set aside to cool for a few minutes. Repeat with second piece of dough.
  14. Serve hot and enjoy!
